# Workshop: Effective Mentoring - Building Meaningful Developmental Relationships

#### <i>A Comprehensive Mentoring Course For Leaders and Emerging Mentors</i>

<p style="border-bottom: 1px solid lightgrey;"></p>

<img style="float: left; margin: 0px 15px 15px 0px;" src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/microsoft/sqlworkshops/master/graphics/textbubble.png"> <h2>About this Workshop</h2>

Welcome to this workshop on *Effective Mentoring: Building Meaningful Developmental Relationships*. In this workshop, you'll learn about the mentoring landscape, the skills and competencies required to be an effective mentor, practical frameworks for structuring mentoring relationships, and techniques for handling complex situations that arise in mentoring partnerships.

The focus of this workshop is to enable professionals to become skilled, confident mentors who can create transformative developmental relationships with their mentees.

You'll start by understanding what mentoring is (and isn't), exploring different mentoring models and paradigms, and identifying your own mentoring philosophy. From there, you will work through modules covering the core competencies of effective mentors, practical session structures and conversation techniques, navigating challenging mentoring scenarios, and creating sustainable mentoring practices. The final module includes a comprehensive Mentee Guide that trained mentors can use to structure their ongoing mentoring relationships.

<img style="float: left; margin: 0px 15px 15px 0px;" src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/microsoft/sqlworkshops/master/graphics/checkmark.png"> <h3>Learning Objectives</h3>

In this workshop you'll learn:
<br>

- The fundamentals of mentoring relationships and how they differ from coaching, managing, and sponsoring
- Core competencies required for effective mentoring, including active listening, powerful questioning, and giving developmental feedback
- Practical frameworks and structures for conducting productive mentoring sessions
- Techniques for navigating challenging situations in mentoring relationships
- How to establish boundaries, manage expectations, and sustain long-term mentoring partnerships

In addition, you will receive a comprehensive **Mentee Guide** that trained mentors can use with their mentees to establish clear expectations, structure sessions, and track progress throughout the mentoring relationship.

The goal of this workshop is to train professionals in the art and science of effective mentoring, enabling them to create developmental relationships that transform careers and lives.

The concepts and skills taught in this workshop form the starting points for:

- Leaders who want to develop mentoring capabilities within their organizations
- Individual contributors preparing to take on mentoring responsibilities
- Human Resources and Learning & Development professionals designing mentoring programs
- Experienced professionals who want to formalize their mentoring approach
- Anyone committed to helping others grow and develop in their careers

<img style="float: left; margin: 0px 15px 15px 0px;" src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/microsoft/sqlworkshops/master/graphics/building1.png"> <h2>Business Applications of this Workshop</h2>

Organizations thrive when they invest in developing their people. Effective mentoring programs contribute to:

- **Improved employee retention**: Employees with mentors are more engaged and more likely to stay with their organizations
- **Accelerated development**: Mentoring transfers knowledge, skills, and organizational wisdom faster than formal training alone
- **Enhanced succession planning**: Mentoring prepares the next generation of leaders and subject matter experts
- **Stronger organizational culture**: Mentoring relationships build connections across organizational boundaries and strengthen culture
- **Knowledge preservation**: Mentoring captures and transfers institutional knowledge before it walks out the door

Studies show that organizations with strong mentoring cultures see measurable improvements in employee satisfaction, productivity, and retention. For individuals, having a mentor is consistently correlated with career advancement, skill development, and professional confidence.

<img style="float: left; margin: 0px 15px 15px 0px;" src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/microsoft/sqlworkshops/master/graphics/owl.png"> <h2>Before Taking this Workshop</h2>

This workshop is designed to be accessible to professionals at all career stages. No prior mentoring experience is required, though participants who have been mentees themselves will have helpful context for the learning.

You should come prepared to:
- Reflect on your own developmental experiences and relationships
- Engage in paired and small group practice exercises
- Discuss real scenarios and challenges from your work environment
- Commit to applying these skills with actual mentees after the workshop

**Materials needed:**
- Notebook or device for taking notes
- Willingness to practice new skills and receive feedback from peers
- (Optional) Examples of situations where you'd like to provide mentorship

If you are new to these foundational concepts, here are a few resources you can review prior to class:

- [What is Mentoring? - Center for Creative Leadership](https://www.ccl.org/articles/leading-effectively-articles/what-is-mentoring/)
- [The Difference Between Coaching and Mentoring - Harvard Business Review](https://hbr.org/)
- [Adult Learning Theory - Malcolm Knowles](https://www.instructionaldesign.org/theories/andragogy/)
- [Active Listening Skills](https://www.mindtools.com/CommSkll/ActiveListening.htm)
